Bake-kit

• scales/measuring cup

• measuring spoons

• mixing bowl

• hand mixer

• rolling pin

• cling film

• cookie cutter

• cookie stamp

• piping bag & nozzle

Time

• 40 m. prep

• 40 m. chilling

• 10 m. baking

Yield

20 noms

Cost

3 out of 5 monies

Difficulty

4 out of 5 biscuits

How to biscuit

1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C

2. Sift all the dry ingredients together and stir.

3. Grate the cold butter and rub into the dry ingredients (should be like buttery bread crumbs)

4. Add flavour and milk so it comes together, tip out onto cling film, and make a flat disc, and put into the fridge to chill for 20 minutes.

5. Turn on to a floured work surface and gently roll out until the dough is between 3 and 5 mm in thickness.

6. Cut out shapes, then stamp each with a decorative motif if desired. Sprinkle with caster sugar and chill in the fridge for a further 20 minutes. Use flat plates to avoid the biscuits bending and separate with cling film if stacking on top of each other so they won’t stick together.

7. Bake in the oven for 10 minutes.

8. For the filling: Melt chocolate then cool a bit.

9. Put soft butter, icing sugar, flavour and custard powder together and use handheld mixer to mix.

10. Pour in cooled chocolate and mix. (If the mixture is too stiff, use the tbsp of milk to loosen).

11. Fill piping bag, and pipe onto the biscuits.

What to biscuit

• 200g butter (chilled)

• 225g plain flour

• 50g icing sugar

• 50g custard powder

• 50g corn flour

• 1/2 tsp baking powder

• 1-2 tbsp milk

• Vanilla extract (good squeeze)

• 8 drops custard flavour

Filling

• 100g white chocolate

• 150g icing sugar

• 150g butter (soft)

• 2 tbsp custard powder

• 20 drops custard flavour

• 1 tbsp milk
