Bake-kit

• scales/measuring cup

• measuring spoons

• mixing bowl

• hand mixer

Time

• 40 m. prep

• 1 hr. chilling

• 10 m. baking

Yield

30 noms

Cost

4 out of 5 monies

Difficulty

3 out of 5 biscuits

How to biscuit

1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C

2. Break up dark chocolate into chunks, and melt in a microwave in short bursts. Leave to cool.

3. Sieve togetherflour, cocoa, and baking powder in a bowl (and milk powder - if using).

4. In a seperate bowl, beat butter and both sugars until fluffy, and beat in syrup. Then add the eggs, one by one.

5. Mix the dry ingredients to the egg mixture, and mix with cooled melted chocolate, and dark chocolate chunks.

6. Add the milk to make a soft dough, don’t overmix.

7. Tip out onto cling film, and use it to gather the dough together, and put into the fridge to chill for 1 hour.

8. Put sparkle powder on a plate and scoop walnut size pieces of the dough, and roll each into a neat ball. Roll just the top half in sparkle.

9. Put biscuits sparkle side up on baking sheets, and space about 10cm apart.

10. Bake in the oven for 8-10 minutes, until the biscuits feel slightly firm when touched.

11. Leave to cool on the trays (as they still need to firm up some more before moving), then cool on a wire rack.

What to biscuit

• 125g soft unsalted butter

• 200g dark chocolate

• 75g soft light brown sugar

• 75g soft dark brown sugar

• 2 tbsp golden syrup

• 2 large eggs

• 150g plain flour

• 2 tbsp milk powder (optional)

• 60g cocoa powder

• 2 tsp baking powder

• 3 tbsp milk

• 100g dark chocolate chunks

• Sparkle powder
