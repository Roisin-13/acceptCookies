Bake-kit

• scales/measuring cup

• measuring spoons

• mixing bowl

• hand mixer

• rolling pin

• cling film

• cookie cutter

• cookie stamp

• piping bag & nozzle

Time

• 40 m. prep

• 40 m. chilling

• 10 m. baking

Yield

20 noms

Cost

3 out of 5 monies

Difficulty

4 out of 5 biscuits

How to biscuit

1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C

2. Break up brown sugar first.

3. Beat butter and both sugars until fluffy, and beat in syrup.

4. Whisk dry ingredients in a bowl, and mix with creamed butter.

5. Use egg to bind, and If a bit dry use a splash of milk (1tbsp max).

6. Tip out onto cling film, and use it to gather the dough together. Make a flat disc, and put into the fridge to chill for 20 minutes.

7. Turn on to a floured work surface and gently roll out until the dough is between 3 and 5 mm in thickness.

8. Cut out shapes, then stamp each with a decorative motif if desired. Sprinkle with caster sugar and chill in the fridge for a further 20 minutes. Use flat plates to avoid the biscuits bending and separate with cling film if stacking on top of each other so they won’t stick together.

9. Bake in the oven for 10 minutes.

10. For the filling: Melt chocolate then cool a bit.

11. Put soft butter, icing sugar and cocoa powder together and use handheld mixer to mix.

12. Pour in cooled chocolate and mix.

13. Fill piping bag, and pipe onto the biscuits.

What to biscuit

• 125g soft unsalted butter

• 80g golden caster sugar

• 20g soft brown sugar

• 2 tbsp golden syrup

• 1 large egg

• 250g plain flour

• 50g cocoa powder

• 1 tsp baking powder

• 1 tbsp milk

Filling

• 75g butter

• 100g chocolate

• 150g icing sugar

• 20g cocoa powder

• 1 tbsp milk
