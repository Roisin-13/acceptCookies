Bake-kit

• scales/measuring cup

• measuring spoons

• mixing bowl

• hand mixer

• rolling pin

• cling film

• bear cookie cutter

• fondant modelling sticks

Time

• 1 hr. prep

• 1 hr. chilling

• 15 m. baking

Yield

50 noms

Cost

2 out of 5 monies

Difficulty

3 out of 5 biscuits

How to biscuit

1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C

2. Beat the brown sugar and butter in the bowl until fluffy.

3. Beat in the egg, then the caramel flavour.

4. Whisk together the flour, baking powder and salt in a seperate bowl, and tip into the butter, mix until combined.

5. Divide the dough in half (so it is easier to work with), wrap in cling film, then pat into discs, and put into the fridge to chill for 30 minutes.

6. Working with 1 piece of dough at a time, turn on to a floured work surface and gently roll out until the dough is between 3 and 5 mm in thickness.

7. Cut out the bears, and then stamp each with ears, eyes, and foot pads. Place a pecan between the arms of each bear and fold the arms up around the pecan to make it look like the bear is hugging the nut and chill in the freezer for 30 minutes. Use flat plates to avoid the biscuits bending and separate with cling film if stacking on top of each other so they won’t stick together.

8. Bake in the oven for 15 minutes, or until golden brown. Cool on a wire rack.

What to biscuit

• 320g plain flour

• 1/4 teaspoon baking powder

• 1/4 teaspoon fine salt

• 150g light brown sugar

• 170g softened unsalted butter

• 1 large egg

• 1/2 tsp caramel flavour

• Pack of pecan halves
